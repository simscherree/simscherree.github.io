    Timeline of Martin Luther King Jr.'s life:

  • January 15, 1929 King is born in Atlanta, Georgia.
  • June 8, 1948 King receives his Bachelor of Arts degree in Sociology from Morehouse College.
  • May 6-8, 1951 King receives his Bachelor of Divinty degree from Crozer Theological Seminar. He delievers the valedicotry address at the commencement ceremony.
  • June 5, 1955 King receives his doctorate in systemetic theology from Boston University.
  • May 17, 1957 King delievers first national address, "Give Us the Ballot" at the Lincoln Memorial in Washington, D.C.
  • September 17, 1958 King's first book Stride Toward Freedom is published.
  • April 16, 1963 King writes his "Letter from Birmingham Jail".
  • August 25, 1963 King delivers "I Have a Dream" speech. More than two thousand demonstrators are at the Lincoln Memorial.
  • January 3, 1964 King is named "Man of the Year" by Time Magazine.
  • December 10, 1964King receives the Nobel peace prize.
  • April 3, 1968 King delivers his final speech "I've Been to the Mountaintop" at a evening rally in Memphis.
  • April 4, 1968 King is shot and killed while standing on the balcony of the Lorraine Motel in Memphis.
